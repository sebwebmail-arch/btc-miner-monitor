1 — Continuity SLA — per Datacenter

Formula: for each 30-min snapshot, compute r = workers_online / workers_total, then apply a quadratic score: score(r) = r².
Continuity SLA = average of all snapshot scores × 100.
This penalises large outages heavily while being forgiving for small offline fractions:
99% online → 98.0% 95% online → 90.3% 90% online → 81.0% 80% online → 64.0% ← threshold 70% online → 49.0% 50% online → 25.0%

2 — Performance SLA — Account level (ATO formula)

Formula (your existing formula, applied per snapshot):
snapshot_SLA = min(100%, total_hr / ATO) — if total HR ≥ ATO, clients get 100%. Otherwise proportional.
period_SLA = average of all snapshot SLAs
Note: this includes downtime (snapshots where HR = 0 score 0%), so it combines availability and throughput in one number.

3 — Performance SLA — per Datacenter (proportional ATO)

Principle: each DC is expected to deliver its proportional share of the ATO commitment.
DC_nominal = ATO × (avg_hr_dc / avg_hr_total_account)
Two separate metrics to avoid confusion:
— Throughput (when online): avg(min(100%, hr_dc / DC_nominal)) for snapshots where hr_dc > 0 only. Measures delivery quality when the DC is up.
— Combined: Continuity SLA × Throughput = overall delivery including downtime.
If Combined > 100% it means this DC compensates for others — capped at 100%.
